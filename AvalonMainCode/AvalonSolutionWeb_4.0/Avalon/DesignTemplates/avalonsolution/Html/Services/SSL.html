What is a SSL Certificate?

SSL (Secure Sockets Layer) is the standard security technology for establishing an encrypted link between a web server and a browser. This link ensures that all data passed between the web server and browsers remain private and integral. SSL is an industry standard and is used by millions of websites for the protection of their online transactions with their customers.

Why do you Need SSL Certificate on your Website?

When accepting payments online you will be asking your customers to transmit very sensitive information across the Web. By its nature the Internet is not secure. Anything transmitted is sent as plain text and can be read by anyone who intercepts the message at any point during the transmission.


(Sources: Synovate/GMI Research, September 2008; Javelin Strategy and Research, March 2009)

Also customers are getting smart about online security. Although most users do not understand the technical aspects of how the Internet works, they do know that their information needs to be protected at all times. More and more of them are looking for the padlock icon and “https” prefix in the address bar of their browser or security provider’s seal on the web page before submitting personal information online. By contrast, if a user attempts to submit personal information to an unsecured Website (i.e., a website that is not protected with a valid SSL Certificate), the browser's built-in security mechanism triggers a warning to the customer, reminding them that the site is not secure and that sensitive data might be intercepted by third parties. Faced with such a warning, most Internet users will likely look elsewhere to make a purchase. Also PayPal and other Integrated Payment Methods require SSL Certificate on your Website compulsorily to process online transactions.

Why HTTPS?

You've probably heard about this, but weren’t sure what it is.

Google wants your website to run using the Secure HTTPS Protocol rather than the common unsecure HTTP Protocol. Why, because HTTPS encryption protects the channel between your customers and your website, ensuring no one in the middle can tamper with the traffic or spy on sensitive data like payment information flowing through the channels. Without this encryption, it's easy for hackers & other busybodies to intercept information sent from your customers computers to your websites or inject malware into your customers computer.

If your website doesn’t run on Secure HTTPS Protocol, visitors will see a "Not Secure" warning while browsing your website. This could have the effect of scaring away your site visitors and raising your bounce rate metrics. You can imagine how badly this could affect your eCommerce sales and demolish your websites reputation among your customers.
